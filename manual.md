# Acid Leak Manual

Acid Leak is a synth-like distortion effect for bass guitar.
For best results, place it at the very beginning of your signal chain, before any buffered effects — ideally directly after your instrument.

## Controls

**Drive** — sets the amount of distortion. To get the full character of the effect, set Drive close to maximum. If your instrument has a particularly hot output, lower settings may also be useful.

**Tone** — controls how much of the high end is rolled off. Lower settings make the effect darker and more focused on low frequencies. Higher settings let more overdrive character and upper harmonics come through.

**Wet** — controls the volume of the effected signal path. When Dry is switched on, the Wet knob sets the balance between the dry signal and the effect. When Dry is switched off, Wet also interacts with the final gain stage, giving you an additional way to shape the response and intensity of the effect.

**Volume** — controls the overall output level. Unity gain, where the volume roughly matches the bypassed signal, is usually found around the middle of the control range, roughly between 11 and 1 o’clock.

**Dry** — switches whether the dry signal is added to the final output. This is not a perfectly clean dry path: it is low-pass filtered and has a slight amount of wet-path bleed, which is part of the character of the circuit. In the On position, you get more clean low-end weight. In the Off position, the pedal focuses more on transistor distortion.

**Footswitch** — true bypass switch. When the effect is off, the LED is off and the signal is not changed or buffered in any way. When the effect is on, the LED lights up and the signal passes through the circuit.

### Connections:

**Power** — 9V DC, center negative. Use a good-quality, regulated power supply designed for guitar effects to avoid unwanted noise or unstable operation. The DC power jack is the standard 2.1 / 5.5 mm barrel connector.

**Input Jack** — the jack on the right side of the enclosure when viewed from above. Connect your instrument here: bass guitar or synthesizer.

**Output Jack** — the jack on the left side of the enclosure when viewed from above. Connect the pedal here to an amplifier, mixer, audio interface, or further effects.
